CAUTION: DO NOT USE PORT FORWARDING!

 

Dear user!

 

Your security and data privacy are particularly important to us.

 

You can use the CCU completely anonymous within your house, but your installation and/or your data will not be communicated on the Internet.

 

Usually, direct access to devices within your network is not possible. The Network Address Translation (NAT) converts the local addresses in your home network into the local address of your router. While NAT was originally developed to enable operation of several or many devices in households with only one address, NAT does also offer protection against abuse from the Internet: With NAT, devices in the users network can typically not be addressed directly. The other way around, NAT can also avoid access from the Internet to your CCU - e.g. with a smartphone.

 

If you want to access your installation with the CCU from the Internet, we strongly recommend the  two following types of solutions:

 

1)       Using a “Remote Access Service”
Here, connection to a rendezvous server on the Internet is established using a small software of the CCU. Select the rendezvous server as well with your smartphone and a corresponding app. Only if the user ID and password are correct, you will be connected with to CCU. Under this method, the CCU is not directly visible from the Internet at any time.

“CloudMatic” and “Orbilon” are two examples for remote access services.
Enclosed to the CCU you will find a list of partner solutions with further details.

2)       Dial-up via VPN (“Virtual Private Network”)
Most router manufacturers allow establishing of a VPN via a smartphone. You can imagine a VPN as secure tunnel, in which all data is encrypted and authenticated. For setting up VPN, at least a user ID and a password are necessary. There are, however, also VPN solutions that require a second kind of authentication, e.g. a TAN that is transmitted to the smartphone. Also with VPN, the CCU is not directly visible from the Internet at any time.

An example for a VPN solution is “My Fritz!” by AVM.

Smartphone apps for the CCU like “Pocket Control App” can automatically support VPNs.

 

 

Why are we warning you against PORT FORWARDING?

 

Approximately 8 years ago, it has been standard to support another technique for remote access: With port forwarding, one or more ports, meaning access points, of a device within a network are made available “outside” at the router. A CCU - or another device in your local network - can be accessed directly with port forwarding from a smartphone on the Internet.

 

In the past, port forwarding has been recommended by may manufacturers and even actively supported to make configuration easier. Until today, there is still a standard “UPnP” that can be used to setup port forwarding for DSL or cable router without the need of manual configuration. LAN and WLAN cameras have been based only on port forwarding in the past and still actively support it until today.

 

The main disadvantage of port forwarding is that your device can be addressed by any other system on the Internet. Unfortunately, also by hackers that are only waiting for using unknown security bugs in Linux to attack the systems in your network.

 

Port forwarding is no longer necessary to access a CCU!

As shown above, there are better alternatives available that are much safer.

 

If you are still using port forwarding for a CCU or other devices,

stop operation AS SOON AS POSSIBLE in your own interest.

 

To further improve the security against unauthorised login, we recommend to deactivate the function "Log in automatically" in the user administration.
